Paneer Tikka Masala
Ingredients

200 grams paneer, cut into cubes, 1/2 cup thick yogurt, 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste, 1 tablespoon lemon juice, 1 teaspoon garam masala, 1 teaspoon cumin powder, 1 teaspoon coriander powder, 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder, 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to your spice preference), 1/2 cup tomato puree or finely chopped tomatoes, 1/4 cup cream or cashew paste (optional, for a creamier sauce), 1 tablespoon oil, Salt to taste, Fresh cilantro (coriander) leaves for garnish, Lemon wedges for serving

Instructions

1In a bowl, mix yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, lemon juice, garam masala, cumin powder, coriander powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt to form the marinade.

2Add the paneer cubes to the marinade, ensuring they are coated evenly. Cover the bowl and let it marinate for at least 30 minutes (or refrigerate for a few hours for better flavor).

3In aPreheat the oven to 200°C (392°F) or heat a grill pan on medium heat. If using the oven, place the marinated paneer cubes on a greased baking tray. If using a grill pan, brush it with oil and place the paneer cubes on it bowl, mix yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, lemon juice, garam masala, cumin powder, coriander powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt to form the marinade.

4Cook the paneer for about 10-12 minutes, turning occasionally, until they develop a slight char and are cooked through.

5In a separate pan, heat oil and add the tomato puree or chopped tomatoes. Cook until the tomatoes are soft and the oil starts to separate.

6Add the cream or cashew paste (if using) and mix well. Cook for another 2-3 minutes.

7Add the cooked paneer cubes to the tomato sauce and gently mix to coat them with the sauce. Simmer for a few minutes to allow the flavors to meld.

8Garnish with fresh cilantro leaves and serve hot with lemon wedges on the side.
